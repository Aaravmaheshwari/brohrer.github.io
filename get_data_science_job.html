1. Get on LinkedIn[link]

  • Send 100 connection requests to data scientists.
  • Share an article from datatau.com that taught you something about a skill.

2. Get on GlassDoor[link]

  • Read three data scientist job descriptions. Note skills that you don’t yet have.
    • Apply for a data scientist position, whether you feel like a fit or not.

3. Refine a skill

  • Pick a skill and use it to build a small demo[link to post]. Don’t spend more than ten hours on it.
  • Put your demo online. Link to it from the Projects section of your LinkedIn profile. Add the skill to your LinkedIn profile if it’s not already there.

4. Interview

  • Talk to every recruiter who contacts you.
  • Participate in every interview you are offered. Note skills that you don’t yet have.

5. Decline any job you’re offered

6. Go to step one and repeat.

Eventually you’ll reach a point, where you can’t quite bring yourself to follow through on step five. It wasn’t exactly what you were looking for but it feels so right, you can’t let it go. Then you’re done.

This method is guaranteed to work! It is a while loop, and the termination criterion is success. Or in the words of John Lennon, “Everything will be okay in the end. If it is not okay, then it is not the end.”
