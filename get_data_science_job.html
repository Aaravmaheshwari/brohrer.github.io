1. Get on LinkedIn

  • Send 100 connection requests to data scientists.
  • Message a friend and tell them you're looking for a data science position.
  • Share an article from datatau.com that taught you something.

2. Get on GlassDoor

  • Read three data scientist job descriptions. Note skills you need to build.
  • Apply for a data scientist position, whether you feel like a fit or not.

3. Build a skill

  • Pick a skill and use it to build a small demo. Don’t spend more than ten hours on it.
  • Put your demo online. Add the skill to your LinkedIn profile and add a link to the Projects section.

4. Interview

  • Only repond to recruiters who contact you by name.
  • Participate in every interview you're offered. Note skills you need to build.

5. Decline every offer

6. Go to step one

 

Eventually you’ll reach a point, where you can’t quite bring yourself to follow through on step five. The position isn’t exactly what you were looking for but it feels so right, you can’t let it go. Then you’re done.

This method is guaranteed to work. It is a while loop, and the termination criterion is success. Or in the words of John Lennon, “Everything will be okay in the end. If it is not okay, then it is not the end.”

Happy hunting!
